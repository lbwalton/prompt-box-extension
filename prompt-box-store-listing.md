### ✨ Full Feature Set

**Text Expansion**
Assign short trigger keywords to any saved snippet. Type the trigger anywhere in Chrome and it expands to the full text instantly. Works in Gmail, LinkedIn, ChatGPT, Notion, Slack, Google Docs — everywhere you type.

**Quick Save**
Right-click any selected text on any webpage > "Save to Prompt Box." Capture a great AI prompt, a useful template, a code snippet — without breaking your flow.

**One-Click Copy**
Open Prompt Box, click any saved item, and it's on your clipboard. Ready to paste anywhere in one motion.

**Smart Organization**
Categorize everything with custom tags and built-in categories. Create new tags inline while saving a prompt, or manage them in Settings. Prompts, templates, snippets, expansions, all in one searchable, sortable library.

**Filter & Sort**
Find exactly what you need by tag, title, date added, or favorites. Zero friction retrieval.

**Favorites**
Star your highest-use prompts and expansions. They surface first so your best material is always one click away.

**Chrome Sync or Local-Only Storage**
Choose where your prompts live. Enable Chrome Sync to automatically sync your library across every Chrome profile signed into the same Google account — no account required. Or keep everything strictly local to a single device. Switch at any time in Settings.

**Import / Export**
Export your entire library to CSV for backup. Import from a spreadsheet to bulk-load your team's shared prompts or migrate from another tool.

**Lightning Search**
Instant search across all saved content. Find any prompt, template, or snippet in under a second.

**Full Edit & Management**
Update, rename, retag, or delete anything at any time. Your library evolves as you do.

### 💡 How It Works

**For AI Prompts:**
1. Highlight useful text on any website
2. Right-click > "Save to Prompt Box"
3. Click the extension icon to browse your library
4. Copy any prompt instantly when you need it

**For Text Expansion:**
1. Save any text you type repeatedly (email, template, snippet)
2. Assign it a short trigger keyword (e.g., `/email`)
3. Type the trigger anywhere in Chrome
4. Watch it expand to the full text automatically
